Hoax Phone Calls Reported in 77 Ridings During 2011 Election

March 1st, 2012

Update 21: Fredericton is added to the list, bringing us to 77.

Update 20: I’m removing Wellington-Halton Hills from the list until either the Liberal Party gets its act together in preparing a complaint, or I get confirmation from an independent source. In the meantime the list keeps growing anyways! New reports flooding in from . Keep sending them to me, and more importantly, keep sending them to Elections Canada. And new word has it the Conservatives are screening evidence from the RMG call centre before it can be turned over to Elections Canada. I’m sure they won’t accidentally shred anything incriminating as they do so.


 

It’s become increasingly clear that there was not one effort to suppress votes during the 2011 election: there were several, some with robocalls, some with live callers, some with just plain idiots participating. Let me make a prediction: within the next couple of weeks, we will hear from several Conservatives claiming they were being scammed too.

Too late. It’s been almost a year now. Bloggers like myself have repeatedly called attention to the issue. Liberal and NDP local offices said they were being victimized. Conservatives weren’t. Frankly, any complaints from that quarter going forward lack credibility.

In any event, I thought it would be helpful to create a list of ridings about which there has been some sort of allegation in the news regarding fake telephone calls during the previous election: calls impersonating another political party, or calls impersonating Elections Canada. This list will be updated regularly. Readers should provide information in the comments about additional ridings I have not got on the list. Those who want to contact me privately can do so at SixthEstateCanada@gmail.com.
